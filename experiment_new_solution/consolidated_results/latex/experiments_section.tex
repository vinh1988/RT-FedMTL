\section{EXPERIMENTS}
\label{sec:experiments}

This section details the experimental evaluation of our proposed Federated Multi-Task Learning (FMTL) approach using Small Language Models (SLMs) \cite{ref7}. We evaluate performance resilience across centralized and decentralized paradigms.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\subsubsection{Datasets and Tasks}
We evaluate on three GLUE tasks: SST-2 (Sentiment), QQP (Paraphrase), and STS-B (Similarity) \cite{ref3}.

\subsubsection{Baselines}
We compare Cent-Single, Cent-Multi, FL-Single, and FL-MTL (Non-IID/IID/LoRA) using FedAvg \cite{ref1}.

\subsection{Discussion: Optimal Model Recommendation}
Based on the full-scale trajectory metrics in Table \ref{tab:full_performance}, we recommend \textbf{DistilBERT} as the optimal SLM backbone for decentralized FMTL. It achieves the highest average resilience across all complexity stages, maintaining a respectable average performance of \textbf{0.8258} in the critical Federated Multi-Task (Non-IID) configuration. Most notably, DistilBERT preserves \textbf{0.6963} on the semantic similarity (STS-B) task under heterogeneity—where architectures like TinyBERT (0.0081) and MiniLM (0.0703) suffer from catastrophic performance failures. This level of cross-task stability combined with a compact transformer architecture makes DistilBERT the most reliable candidate for practical, privacy-preserving NLU deployments.
