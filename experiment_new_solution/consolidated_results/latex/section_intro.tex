\section{Introduction} \label{sec:introduction}
Natural language understanding (NLU) is a core module in artificial intelligence systems such as social media monitoring \cite{arunkumar2025ai}, conversational agents \cite{manzo2025artificial}, information retrieval \cite{liu2025robust}, and so on. According to the modern approach, these systems have achieved impressive performance through large-scale language models \cite{devlin2019bert}. However, they typically rely on a centralized training and deployment process which introduces challenges concerning data privacy, computational cost, and inference latency. 

In recent years, small language models (SLMs) \cite{sanh2019distilbert, turc2019well, wang2020minilm} have emerged as a potential alternative to large models because of that they have low inference latency and cost-effectiveness. Therefore, these models are appropriate in resource-constrained environments, addressing the challenges of large language models \cite{li2020federated}. Notwithstanding, SLMs are often limited in capacity, making it hard to maintain performance for a variety of natural language processing (NLP) tasks under independent training. 

Parallel to the development of SLMs, multi-task learning (MTL) \cite{ruder2017overview, caruana1997multitask} has become as a powerful paradigm to tackle multiple tasks simultaneously. This approach of leveraging useful information from related tasks can lead to better learning performance \cite{wang2018glue, yu2020gradient}. Even so, most existing MTL methods generally require centralized data for training, which is impractical in privacy-sensitive contexts where data is distributed across multiple clients. 

At the same time, federated learning (FL) has been applied successfully to domains where data privacy and ownership are critical, such as healthcare~\cite{dayan2021federated} and finance~\cite{long2020federated}. This is a distributed architecture that  enables model training across clients without transferring raw data to a central server, thereby preserving privacy while supporting large-scale learning \cite{mcmahan2017communication, yang2019federated}. 
However, integrating FL with MTL raises significant challenges in terms of communication overhead and inference latency. Furthermore, existing federated NLP frameworks \cite{rani2025privacy, kumar2025federated, cai2023efficient} rarely consider real-time constraints, while this is a very important factor for latency-sensitive applications such as health monitoring \cite{sharma2025smart}, chatbots \cite{kanagajothi2025enhancing}, and personalized assistants \cite{mok2025exploring}. 
These challenges motivate the development of real-time federated MTL framework for NLU that reduces communication overhead and inference latency.

In this work, we propose RT-FedMTL, a novel real-time federated MTL framework with SLMs for efficient and privacy-preserving NLU. Our framework unifies FL and MTL to enable collaborative training across distributed clients while preserving data locality and leveraging shared representations across tasks. Additionally, we utilize WebSocket protocols and SLMs tuning to reduce the feedback loop and provide near-instantaneous global updates. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work to explore federated MTL with SLMs for real-time NLU. 
Our key contributions are summarized as follows:
 \begin{enumerate}
     \item We propose a novel real-time federated MTL framework with SLMs for efficient and privacy-preserving NLU. This framework harnesses cross-task knowledge transfer without causing a large amount of resources to be consumed on devices.
     \item We assess five SLM architectures (DistilBERT \cite{sanh2019distilbert}, BERT-Medium \cite{turc2019well}, BERT-Mini \cite{turc2019well}, MiniLM \cite{wang2020minilm}, and TinyBERT \cite{jiao2019tinybert}) with respect to three standard NLU tasks (sentiment analysis, paraphrase detection, and semantic similarity), to get the best trade-off between performance and resource usage.
     \item We investigate the effects of non-independent identically distribution (non-IID) and task heterogeneity on convergence speed and task accuracy in real-time domain.
     \item We demonstrate RT-FedMTL can drastically minimize per-client GPU memory requirements (up to 74-93\%) versus centralized multi-task learning baselines, while keeping performance for multiple benchmarks.
 \end{enumerate}
