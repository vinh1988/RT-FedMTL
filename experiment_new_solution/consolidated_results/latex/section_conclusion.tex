\section{CONCLUSION}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Herein we reported a broad study into RT-FedMTL with SLMs on various NLU benchmarks. With our systematic assessment across five architectures, we derived key valuable ideas for implementing decentralized NLU systems. The findings here show the successful balancing of protecting user privacy with a strong optimization of resource usage, with FL achieving up to 93\% decrease in per-client GPU resource usage. We discovered a key trade-off in performance and efficiency: for sentiment analysis, DistilBERT delivered consistently the best performance, being notably robust to negative transfer in SST-2. However, for a unified multi-task ensemble, MiniLM proves to be the most flexible and effective architecture and outperformed the larger models in QQP and STS-B tasks, at around 3$\times$ lower memory footprint (0.38\,GB compared to 1.09\,GB). In the case where memory and bandwidth are the bottleneck, architectures such as MiniLM represent the best balance of stability and throughput for real-time federated multi-task scenarios. For further investigations adaptive task-sharing mechanisms will be explored to tackle negative transfer in multi-head architectures. In addition, we further discuss personalized federated learning techniques and the possibility of differentially private mechanisms implemented in the real-time communication layer, allowing the efficiency gains not to come at the cost of the privacy in the production NLU deployments.
