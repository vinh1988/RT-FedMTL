\begin{abstract}
The adoption of Federated Learning (FL) for privacy-preserving Natural Language Understanding (NLU) is often hindered by the resource constraints of edge devices and the complexity of multi-task environments. This paper proposes a Real-Time Federated Multi-Task Learning framework using Small Language Models (RT-FMTL-SLM). We systematically evaluate five SLM architectures across benchmarks from the GLUE suite under multiple paradigm-complexity stages and data distributions. Our findings reveal that RT-FMTL-SLM achieves a per-client resource reduction of 74--93\% relative to centralized training, though it incurs a 2.2--3.7$\times$ training time overhead due to real-time synchronization. We further analyze the trade-offs of Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA), which reduces communication volume by over 90\% but challenges regression stability in tasks like STS-B. DistilBERT emerges as the most robust architecture for multi-task stability under heterogeneous conditions. The source code is available at: \url{https://github.com/vinh1988/RT-FMTL-SLM.git}.
\end{abstract}
