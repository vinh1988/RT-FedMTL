\begin{abstract}
The demand for privacy-preserving Natural Language Understanding (NLU) has led to the rapid adoption of Federated Learning (FL). However, real-time multi-task deployments on edge devices face significant challenges regarding communication efficiency and data heterogeneity. In this paper, we propose a Real-Time Federated Multi-Task Learning framework using Small Language Models (RT-FMTL-SLM). We systematically evaluate our approach across five diverse SLM architectures using benchmarks from the GLUE suite (SST-2, QQP, and STS-B) under multiple paradigm-complexity stages, including IID and Non-IID data distributions. Our results demonstrate that RT-FMTL-SLM achieves a significant reduction in per-client resource consumption by \textbf{74--93\%} relative to centralized baselines. While federated synchronization introduces a \textbf{2.2--3.7$\times$} training time overhead due to real-time coordination, we observe a critical performance-efficiency trade-off with Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA); although it reduces communication volume by over \textbf{90\%}, it leads to substantial performance degradation in regression tasks like STS-B. We identify DistilBERT as the most robust architecture for multi-task stability under heterogeneous conditions. The source code and implementation are available at: \url{https://github.com/vinh1988/RT-FMTL-SLM.git}.
\end{abstract}
