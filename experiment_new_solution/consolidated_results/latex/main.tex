\begin{abstract}
The adoption of Federated Learning (FL) for privacy-preserving Natural Language Understanding (NLU) is often hindered by the resource constraints of edge devices and the complexity of Multi-Task Learning (MTL) environments. This paper proposes a Real-Time Federated Multi-Task Learning framework using Small Language Models (SLMs), termed RT-FedMTL. We systematically evaluate five SLM architectures across benchmarks from the General Language Understanding Evaluation (GLUE) suite under multiple paradigm-complexity stages and data distributions. Our findings reveal that RT-FedMTL achieves a per-client resource reduction of 74--93\% relative to centralized training, though it incurs a 2.2--3.7$\times$ training time overhead due to real-time synchronization. We identify a critical architectural trade-off: while DistilBERT offers the highest robustness for sentiment analysis, MiniLM achieves an optimal balance, matching or exceeding larger models in MTL benchmarks like QQP and STS-B with a 3$\times$ smaller memory footprint. We further demonstrate that Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) effectively mitigates communication bottlenecks in this real-time setting. The source code is available at: \url{https://github.com/vinh1988/RT-FedMTL.git}.
\end{abstract}
